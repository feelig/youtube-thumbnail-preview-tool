Utah

Utah annual renewal fee and deadline

Use this page if you need the Utah annual report or renewal due date. Start with the anniversary date and then confirm whether the entity is domestic or foreign before you rely on the reinstatement path.

At a glance

Main due rule The renewal is due one year from registration and then each year after that.
Standard renewal fee $18 for corporations, nonprofits, LLCs, LPs, and LLPs on the current fee schedule.
Late and reinstatement rule $10 late renewal fee, and Utah says foreign entities cannot use the reinstatement path if they lapse.

Corporation or LLC Use the anniversary-date renewal lane

Utah corporations and LLCs use the annual renewal tied to the registration anniversary, with the current $18 renewal fee on the published fee schedule.

LP or LLP Use the same annual cycle, but keep the entity type straight

Utah limited partnerships and LLPs also renew on the annual cycle, and the fee schedule keeps them in the standard renewal-fee lane.

Foreign or lapsed entity Check status before assuming reinstatement is available

Utah's reinstatement FAQ says foreign entities cannot reinstate. If the record is already lapsed, confirm the exact status before you pay.

Question What due date should I start with for Utah?

Start with One year from registration and annually thereafter, with notice timing tied to the anniversary date. If the state uses anniversary, report-month, or fiscal-year timing, the official state record still controls.

Question What fee or amount does this page show?

This page starts with $18 renewal fee for corporations, nonprofits, LLCs, LPs, and LLPs, plus a $10 late renewal fee. If the amount changes by entity type, filing method, or status, confirm it on the official state source before paying.

Question What if I am already late?

$10 late renewal fee, and Utah says foreign entities cannot use the reinstatement path if they lapse. Confirm the current status and amount on the official state record before you pay or file.
