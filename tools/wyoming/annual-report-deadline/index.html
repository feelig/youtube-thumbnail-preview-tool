Wyoming

Wyoming annual report fee and due date

Use this page if you need the Wyoming annual report deadline or annual license tax rule. Most customers start with the first day of the anniversary month, then confirm whether the entity uses the $60 minimum license tax or one of Wyoming's flat annual report fees.

Recently checked Checked: April 18, 2026 Reviewed by FinLogic Hub Editorial Team 5 official sources Source: Wyoming Secretary of State, Business Division

At a glance

Main due date Annual report is due on or before the first day of the anniversary month, starting in the first anniversary year.
Most profit entities Annual license tax is $60 or two-tenths of one mill on the dollar ($.0002) of Wyoming assets, whichever is greater.
Reminder and late path Courtesy reminder emails go out 60, 30, and 10 days before the due date. Missing the report makes the entity delinquent and later administratively dissolved.

What to know first

What to check before you file in Wyoming

Entity type Annual report fee What to watch
Profit corporation, LLC, LP, or LLP $60 or $.0002 of Wyoming assets, whichever is greater Most profit entities use Wyoming's annual license tax rather than a flat universal filing fee.
Nonprofit corporation $25 Wyoming treats nonprofits as a flat-fee annual report filing.
Statutory trust or statutory foundation $100 Wyoming's FAQ and fee schedule put statutory trusts and statutory foundations in the $100 flat-fee lane.

What most filers need to know first

These answers keep the page short while still covering the questions people ask before they file.

Question Who should use this Wyoming guide?

Use this page for LLCs, profit corporations, LPs, LLPs, nonprofits, statutory trusts, and statutory foundations. Match the entity type on the official state record before you rely on the summary.

Question What due date should I start with for Wyoming?

Start with Due each year on or before the first day of the anniversary month; report may be filed up to 120 days early. If the state uses anniversary, report-month, or fiscal-year timing, the official state record still controls.

Question What fee or amount does this page show?

This page starts with $60 minimum for most profit entities or .0002 of Wyoming assets, $25 nonprofits, and $100 statutory trusts or foundations. If the amount changes by entity type, filing method, or status, confirm it on the official state source before paying.

Question What if I am already late?

Missed filings make the entity delinquent, then administratively dissolved; reinstatement requires a $100 fee plus each delinquent annual report fee. Confirm the current status and amount on the official state record before you pay or file.
