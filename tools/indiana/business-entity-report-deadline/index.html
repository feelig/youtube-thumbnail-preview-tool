Indiana

Indiana business entity report fee and due date

Use this page if you need the Indiana business entity report due date or filing fee. The first step is knowing this is a biennial Secretary of State filing, not a tax payment, and the report is tied to the anniversary month of formation or registration.

At a glance

Filing cadence First report due two years after formation or registration, then every other year.
Main fee $32 on INBiz or $50 by paper for for-profit entities.
Late-state rule No flat late fee is listed on the main report page, but missing the report can lead to administrative dissolution or revocation.

Pick your case first

Start with the entity type that matches your record

Use the matching case below before you rely on a fee, filing path, or due date.

For-profit business Use the INBiz fee and anniversary-month rule

Indiana for-profit business entity reports are due every other year by the end of the anniversary month, with the published $32 INBiz fee or $50 paper fee.

Nonprofit business Use the separate nonprofit fee lane

Indiana nonprofits still file the same biennial report, but the main report page uses the lower $22 INBiz or $20 paper fee lane.

Past-due or dissolved record Move out of the normal filing lane quickly

Once Indiana has already administratively dissolved or revoked the business, the ordinary report answer is not enough by itself and reinstatement steps may be required.

Quick questions

What most filers need to know first

These answers keep the page short while still covering the questions people ask before they file.

Question Who should use this Indiana guide?

Use this page for Corporations, LLCs, nonprofit corporations, LPs, LLPs, benefit corporations, business trusts, and agricultural cooperatives. Match the entity type on the official state record before you rely on the summary.

Question What due date should I start with for Indiana?

Start with First report due two years after formation or registration; then every other year by the end of the anniversary month. If the state uses anniversary, report-month, or fiscal-year timing, the official state record still controls.

Question What fee or amount does this page show?

This page starts with $32 on INBiz or $50 by paper for for-profit entities, or $22 on INBiz and $20 by paper for nonprofits. If the amount changes by entity type, filing method, or status, confirm it on the official state source before paying.

Question What if I am already late?

No flat late fee is listed on the main filing page, but missed reports can lead to administrative dissolution or revocation. Confirm the current status and amount on the official state record before you pay or file.
