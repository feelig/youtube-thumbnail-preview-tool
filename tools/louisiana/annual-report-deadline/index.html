Louisiana

Louisiana annual report fee and due date

Use this page if you need the Louisiana annual report deadline or fee. Start with the renewal date shown on the business record, because Louisiana only opens the online annual report within 30 days of that renewal date.

At a glance

Core filing window Louisiana only opens online annual report filing within 30 days of the renewal date.
Common fee lane $30 for most corporations, LLCs, and partnerships, or $10 for domestic nonprofit annual reports.
Revocation path Louisiana entities can be revoked after three missed annual reports, while foreign entities can be revoked after one missed annual report.

Pick your case first

Start with the entity type that matches your record

Use the matching case below before you rely on a fee, filing path, or due date.

Domestic corporation or LLC Most Louisiana domestic annual reports start with the $30 lane

Domestic business corporations and LLCs commonly use the $30 annual report lane, but the filing can only be opened in the 30-day window tied to the renewal date.

Domestic nonprofit Lower fee, but the annual report still has to be filed

Louisiana domestic nonprofit corporations use the lower $10 annual report fee, and the state still expects the report every year even if nothing changed.

Foreign corporation or LLC The fee may be the same, but the late risk is not

Foreign corporations and foreign LLCs still use the $30 annual report lane, but Louisiana says foreign entities can be revoked after one missed annual report.

Revocation notice A mailed notice means the cleanup clock is already running

Louisiana's FAQ says a notice of intent to revoke generally gives Louisiana entities 30 days to respond and foreign corporations or LLCs 60 days.
