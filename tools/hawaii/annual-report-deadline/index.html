Hawaii

Hawaii annual business report fee and deadline

Use this page if you need the Hawaii annual business report due date or fee. Start with the registration quarter, because Hawaii files by quarter rather than one fixed statewide day, and a newly registered entity generally does not file the report in that same year.

At a glance

Quarter-based timing The report is due by the end of the calendar quarter tied to the entity's registration date: March 31, June 30, September 30, or December 31.
Common online fee lane $12.50 online for LLCs, profit corporations, and LLPs; $2.50 for nonprofits; and $5.00 for partnerships.
Late cost Delinquent Hawaii annual business reports can still be filed online, with a $10.00 late fee assessed per delinquent year.

Pick your case first

LLC, profit corporation, or LLP Most Hawaii business filers use the $12.50 online lane

Hawaii LLCs, profit corporations, and LLPs generally file their annual business reports during the due quarter using the common online fee lane.

Nonprofit corporation Nonprofits still file annually, but the fee lane is lower

Hawaii nonprofits follow the same quarter-based timing rule but use the lower online filing amount published by the Business Registration Division.

Partnership Partnerships use the same quarter rule with their own fee

Hawaii partnerships still track the registration quarter, but the published annual statement fee differs from the corporation and LLC lane.

New or delinquent filing The first year and late years need different handling

If the entity registered in the same year the report would otherwise be due, Hawaii says that filing is not required that year, but older delinquent years add a $10 late fee per year.
