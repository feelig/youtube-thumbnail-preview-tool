Maine

Maine annual report fee and due date

Use this page if you need the Maine annual report deadline or fee. Most businesses start with the June 1 due date, then confirm whether the entity uses the domestic, foreign, or nonprofit fee.

Recently checked Checked: April 18, 2026 Reviewed by FinLogic Hub Editorial Team 5 official sources Source: Maine Secretary of State, Bureau of Corporations, Elections and Commissions

At a glance

Legal deadline Annual report must be delivered between January 1 and June 1.
Common fee split $85 for domestic business entities, $150 for foreign business entities, and $35 for domestic or foreign nonprofit corporations.
Reminder rule Maine does not mail annual report reminders, but it sends courtesy email reminders to the address on file.

Quick questions

What most filers need to know first

These answers keep the page short while still covering the questions people ask before they file.

Question Who should use this Maine guide?

Use this page for Corporations, LLCs, LPs, LLPs, and nonprofit corporations. Match the entity type on the official state record before you rely on the summary.

Question What due date should I start with for Maine?

Start with First report due the year after filing; then every year between January 1 and June 1. If the state uses anniversary, report-month, or fiscal-year timing, the official state record still controls.

Question What fee or amount does this page show?

This page starts with $85 for domestic business entities, $150 for foreign business entities, and $35 for nonprofit corporations. If the amount changes by entity type, filing method, or status, confirm it on the official state source before paying.

Question What if I am already late?

Late filing penalty is assessed after June 1, and unpaid penalties can lead to administrative dissolution or revocation. Confirm the current status and amount on the official state record before you pay or file.
