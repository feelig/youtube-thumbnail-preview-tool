West Virginia

West Virginia annual report fee and deadline

Use this page if you need the West Virginia annual report deadline or fee. Start with the entity type, because standard corporations and LLCs use the $25 annual report lane, while West Virginia LLPs use a separate annual notice.

At a glance

Core filing window January 1 through June 30 each year following the calendar year in which the business was registered.
Standard fee $25 annual report fee, plus a $1 West Virginia portal fee for online filings.
Late and status risk Late fees can apply after June 30, and missed reports can lead to noncompliance, administrative dissolution, or revocation.

Pick your case first

Start with the entity type that matches your record

Use the matching case below before you rely on a fee, filing path, or due date.

Profit corporation or LLC Most standard West Virginia filers start with the $25 lane

West Virginia corporations and LLCs generally use the standard annual report lane, with the filing window open from January 1 through June 30.

Nonprofit corporation The filing window stays the same, but the late-fee path is lighter

West Virginia nonprofits still file the annual report on the same calendar, but the published late-fee schedule separates nonprofit organizations from profit organizations.

LLP LLPs do not use this standard annual report lane

West Virginia LLPs use an annual notice instead of the standard annual report and the published annual amount is much higher than the typical business filing.

Already late Good standing starts to disappear once the June window closes

West Virginia says late filing is still better than never filing, because missed reports can leave the business out of compliance and exposed to dissolution or revocation.

Quick questions

What most filers need to know first

These answers keep the page short while still covering the questions people ask before they file.

Question Who should use this West Virginia guide?

Use this page for Corporations, LLCs, nonprofits, LPs, business trusts, and voluntary associations; LLPs use a separate annual notice. Match the entity type on the official state record before you rely on the summary.

Question What due date should I start with for West Virginia?

Start with January 1 to June 30 each year following the calendar year in which the business was registered. If the state uses anniversary, report-month, or fiscal-year timing, the official state record still controls.

Question What fee or amount does this page show?

This page starts with $25 annual report fee, with late fees after June 30. If the amount changes by entity type, filing method, or status, confirm it on the official state source before paying.

Question What if I am already late?

$50 late fee for profit organizations or $25 late fee for nonprofits, plus dissolution or revocation risk. Confirm the current status and amount on the official state record before you pay or file.
