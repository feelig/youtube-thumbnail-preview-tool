Pennsylvania annual report deadline

Pennsylvania's annual report requirement started in 2025. The Department of State now uses different filing windows by association type, with a $7 filing fee for most business corporations, LLCs, LPs, and LLPs.

Last reviewed: March 11, 2026 Source: Pennsylvania Department of State

At a glance

LLC filing window January 1 through September 30.
Standard filing fee $7 for most business corporations, LLCs, LPs, and LLPs.
Late enforcement timing Administrative action begins with annual reports due in 2027.

Deadline table

Pennsylvania filing windows by association type

Association type Filing dates Published fee
Corporations, business and nonprofit, domestic and foreign January 1 to June 30 $7 for business corporations, $0 for nonprofits
Limited liability companies, domestic and foreign January 1 to September 30 $7, or $0 for LLCs with a not-for-profit purpose
Limited partnerships, LLPs, business trusts, professional associations January 1 to December 31 $7, or $0 for qualifying not-for-profit entities

What happens if you fail to file

The Pennsylvania Department of State says failure to file will subject the association to administrative dissolution, termination, or cancellation and loss of protection of its name. However, Act 122 includes a transition period. The state says entities that fail to file annual reports in the 2027 calendar year will be subject to enforcement six months after the due date.

Reinstatement note

Domestic entities can reinstate after administrative dissolution or cancellation, but registered foreign associations generally must reregister instead of curing retroactively through reinstatement.
