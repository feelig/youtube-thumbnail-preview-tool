California

California Statement of Information fee and due date

Use this page if you need the California Statement of Information deadline or fee. Most businesses start with the 90-day initial filing rule, then confirm whether the entity follows the corporation schedule or the LLC every-two-years schedule.

Recently checked Checked: March 17, 2026 Reviewed by FinLogic Hub Editorial Team 6 official sources Source: California Secretary of State

At a glance

Stock corporation fee $25 for the Statement of Information.
California LLC fee $20 for the Statement of Information.
Initial filing rule Due within 90 days of initial registration for both stock corporations and LLCs.

What to know first

What to check before you file in California

Entity type Initial due date Recurring rule Filing fee
California stock corporation or qualified out-of-state corporation Within 90 days of initial registration Every year in the month of registration $25
California LLC or qualified out-of-state LLC Within 90 days of initial registration Every two years in the month of registration, in the matching odd or even year $20
