Alaska

Alaska biennial report fee and deadline

Use this page if you need the Alaska biennial report due date or filing fee. Start with the entity type, because Alaska uses a January lane for most for-profit businesses and LLCs, a July lane for nonprofits and cooperatives, and a separate initial-report rule for several domestic entities.

At a glance

January lane For-profit corporations, professional corporations, LLCs, and LLPs file by January 2 in the matching odd or even year, with late fees after February 1.
July lane Nonprofit, religious, and cooperative corporations file by July 2 in the matching odd or even year, with late fees after August 1.
Common fee split $100 domestic or $200 foreign for many January biennial reports, while nonprofit biennial reports use the $25 lane.

Pick your case first

Start with the entity type that matches your record

Use the matching case below before you rely on a fee, filing path, or due date.

Domestic corporation, LLC, or LLP Most domestic Alaska business filers use the January lane

Domestic business corporations, professional corporations, LLCs, and LLPs generally file by January 2 in the matching odd or even year and use the $100 biennial report lane.

Foreign corporation, LLC, or LLP Foreign Alaska filers use the same date but a higher fee

Foreign business corporations, foreign LLCs, and foreign LLPs also use the January biennial report cycle, but the official fee lane is higher than the domestic amount.

Nonprofit, religious, or cooperative corporation The Alaska nonprofit lane uses July, not January

Nonprofit, religious, and cooperative corporations file in July of the matching odd or even year, with the lower nonprofit fee lane and a separate cooperative amount.

Initial report or reinstatement The first Alaska filing or a missed report can change the answer

Several domestic Alaska entities file a free initial report within six months after creation, while entities that miss one or more biennial reports can move into involuntary dissolution or revocation and need reinstatement work.

What to know first

What to check before you file in Alaska

Entity type Due rule Published amount What to watch
Domestic business corporation, professional corporation, LLC, or LLP January 2 of the matching odd or even year; filing opens October 2 of the prior year $100.00, or $137.50 on or after February 2 Domestic Alaska entities can also have a free initial report within six months after creation.
Foreign business corporation, foreign LLC, or foreign LLP January 2 of the matching odd or even year; filing opens October 2 of the prior year $200.00, or $247.50 on or after February 2 Foreign entities do not file the free initial report.
Nonprofit or religious corporation July 2 of the matching odd or even year; filing opens April 2 $25.00, or $30.00 on or after August 2 This is the most common Alaska summer filing lane.
Cooperative corporation July 2 of the matching odd or even year; filing opens April 2 $100.00, or $110.00 on or after August 2 Cooperatives share the summer lane but not the nonprofit fee.

Quick questions

What most filers need to know first

These answers keep the page short while still covering the questions people ask before they file.

Question Who should use this Alaska guide?

Use this page for Domestic and foreign corporations, LLCs, LLPs, nonprofits, religious corporations, and cooperatives. Match the entity type on the official state record before you rely on the summary.

Question What due date should I start with for Alaska?

Start with January 2 or July 2 in the matching odd or even year, depending on entity type. If the state uses anniversary, report-month, or fiscal-year timing, the official state record still controls.

Question What fee or amount does this page show?

This page starts with $100 domestic or $200 foreign for many January biennial reports, or $25 for nonprofit July filers. If the amount changes by entity type, filing method, or status, confirm it on the official state source before paying.

Question What if I am already late?

Late fees start after February 1 and missed reports can push the entity toward involuntary dissolution or revocation. Confirm the current status and amount on the official state record before you pay or file.
