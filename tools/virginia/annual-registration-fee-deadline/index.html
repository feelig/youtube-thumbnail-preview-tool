Virginia

Virginia annual registration fee and annual report deadline

Use this page if you need the Virginia recurring filing rule. The first step is matching the entity type, because corporations, LLCs, LPs, business trusts, and LLPs do not all use the same filing label or due date.

Recently checked Checked: March 17, 2026 Reviewed by FinLogic Hub Editorial Team 6 official sources Source: Virginia State Corporation Commission

At a glance

Corporation annual report Due the last day of the month of incorporation or Virginia registration, with no report fee.
LLC annual registration fee $50 due the last day of the month the LLC was organized or registered.
LLP or LLLP continuation report Due July 1 each year with a $50 filing fee.

Quick questions

What most filers need to know first

These answers keep the page short while still covering the questions people ask before they file.

Question Who should use this Virginia guide?

Use this page for Corporations, LLCs, LPs, business trusts, LLPs, and LLLPs. Match the entity type on the official state record before you rely on the summary.

Question What due date should I start with for Virginia?

Start with Last day of formation month for corporations and LLCs, October 1 for LPs and business trusts, or July 1 for LLPs and LLLPs. If the state uses anniversary, report-month, or fiscal-year timing, the official state record still controls.

Question What fee or amount does this page show?

This page starts with $50 for most non-corporate recurring filings; corporation fee varies by share count. If the amount changes by entity type, filing method, or status, confirm it on the official state source before paying.

Question What if I am already late?

Loss of good standing, revocation, or cancellation depending on entity type. Confirm the current status and amount on the official state record before you pay or file.
