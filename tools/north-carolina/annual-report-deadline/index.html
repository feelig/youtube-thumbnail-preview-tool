North Carolina

North Carolina annual report deadline for LLCs and corporations

The North Carolina Secretary of State publishes different annual report timing and fee rules by entity type. LLCs and L3Cs use a fixed April 15 deadline with a $200 filing fee plus a small electronic fee, while business corporations follow the 15th day of the fourth month after fiscal year end and use a much lower annual report fee.

At a glance

LLC or L3C deadline April 15 each year after the year of creation.
LLC fee $200 paper, $202 by ACH, or $203 by credit card online.
Corporation fee $25 paper, $20 by ACH, or $21 by credit card online.

North Carolina is one of the states where the online total changes slightly depending on whether the payment is made by ACH or credit card.

How to read the rule

North Carolina gets easier once you separate entity type from payment method

How to read the deadline correctly

LLCs in North Carolina are simpler than corporations because they use a fixed April 15 due date. Business corporations do not use that fixed date because the filing deadline tracks the entity's fiscal year end instead.

What happens if you are late

The annual report due-date chart confirms when the filing is due and what the filing fee is, but it does not publish a flat late fee on the same page. If you are already overdue, check the Secretary of State filing instructions before relying on any third-party summary.

If the business was dissolved already

The North Carolina reinstatement page says that when the ground for dissolution is failure to file annual reports, the business must file an annual report and pay the applicable filing fee for each missing year. Overdue entities should not stop at the current-year fee alone.

Where the fee math comes from

North Carolina separates the statutory filing fee from the electronic payment fee. That is why the best planning answer is usually a paper amount plus a small online add-on for ACH or credit-card processing.
