North Carolina

North Carolina annual report fee and deadline for LLCs and corporations

Use this page if you need the North Carolina annual report fee or deadline. LLCs and L3Cs usually file by April 15 with a $203 online fee or $200 paper fee, while corporations use a fiscal-year deadline.

At a glance

LLC or L3C deadline April 15 each year after the year of creation.
LLC fee $200 paper, $202 by ACH, or $203 by credit card online.
Corporation fee $25 paper, $20 by ACH, or $21 by credit card online.

Pick your case first

Start with the entity type that matches your record

Use the matching case below before you rely on a fee, filing path, or due date.

LLC or L3C Most LLCs start with the April 15 deadline

Use the LLC or L3C rule if the business is domestic or foreign in that category, then confirm whether you are paying by paper, ACH, or card.

Corporation Use the fiscal-year deadline instead

North Carolina corporations do not follow the LLC April 15 rule by default, so check the fiscal-year timing before you quote a due date.

Partnership or other record Do not default to the LLC fee

Some partnerships and foreign registrations use different rules or totals, so match the entity on the SOS record before you rely on the LLC number.

Question What due date should I start with for North Carolina?

Start with April 15 for LLCs; fiscal-year-based for corporations. If the state uses anniversary, report-month, or fiscal-year timing, the official state record still controls.

Question What fee or amount does this page show?

Question What if I am already late?
