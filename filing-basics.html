Basics

Annual report vs franchise tax vs annual registration

One of the biggest reasons business owners land on the wrong page is that states do not use one shared label for the same kind of yearly compliance task. Some states call it an annual report. Others use annual registration, annual list, annual tax, or franchise tax report. Those words are not interchangeable, and the wrong label can send you to the wrong filing portal or make you rely on the wrong due date.

FinLogic Hub keeps these labels separate because the distinction changes the answer. In Delaware, for example, a domestic corporation files an annual report and pays franchise tax, while a Delaware LLC does not file an annual report and instead pays a separate annual tax. In Texas, many entities focus on the franchise tax report plus the correct information report rather than a standard annual report page.

Quick difference table

Label What it usually means Example on this site Main risk if you mix it up
Annual report A yearly filing that confirms entity information or continued existence with the state. North Carolina and Pennsylvania You may miss a filing window or rely on the wrong fee schedule.
Annual registration A yearly entity registration update that serves a similar recurring-compliance role but uses a different state label. Georgia You may search for "annual report" and never reach the right Georgia portal.
Annual list A yearly list filing that may be paired with another renewal fee instead of standing alone. Nevada You may overlook the separate state business license renewal fee.
Annual tax A recurring state charge that may apply even when there is no annual report for that entity type. Delaware LLC annual tax You may think no action is due because you searched only for "annual report."
Franchise tax report A yearly tax filing that can come with separate information-report requirements and tax thresholds. Texas You may miss the information report or misunderstand no-tax-due filing rules.

How to use the right page

  • Start with the state name, then identify the exact entity type.
  • Check whether the state treats domestic and foreign entities differently.
  • Look for the actual label used by the state, not the one used in a third-party article.
  • Confirm whether the recurring obligation is a filing, a tax, a registration, or a combination.
  • Use the official filing portal or statute linked on the page before submitting anything.

Why this matters for costs

The filing label affects cost as much as timing. A page that talks only about an annual report filing fee may miss a second recurring charge, like a business-license renewal, or a tax amount that is due even without a report. That is why FinLogic Hub keeps fee components separate whenever the state does.
