About FinLogic Hub

FinLogic Hub focuses on a narrower promise: explain selected state filing deadlines, annual report fees, and recurring business-entity charges with clear sourcing and simple language. The goal is not to publish hundreds of thin pages. The goal is to build a smaller set of useful pages that help readers find the right deadline, fee, and official filing source more quickly.

Every live guide starts from an official state source. For some states that means a Secretary of State deadline chart. For others it means an annual report page, a filing portal, or a statute that controls annual list fees and late-payment penalties. When the state source is clear, the guide summarizes it in plain language. When the state source does not clearly publish a flat late fee or reinstatement cost, the guide says that directly instead of guessing.

The live pages also separate entity types instead of blending them into one number. That matters in states like Delaware, where domestic corporations file annual reports while Delaware LLCs pay an annual tax instead, and in Nevada, where annual list fees and state business license renewal fees are separate line items.

Review dates are included because this subject changes. If a state updates its online filing fee or changes when enforcement begins, the page needs to be refreshed. That is also why these pages are informational only. They are a reading aid, not a replacement for the filing instructions, a registered agent, or legal advice.

Editorial rules for live pages

  • Use official state sources for deadlines, filing fees, and late-payment language.
  • Show the review date on the page.
  • Link back to the official filing page or statute.
  • Say "not clearly stated" when the official guidance does not publish a number.
  • Remove unsupported or outdated pages instead of keeping uncertain information live.
