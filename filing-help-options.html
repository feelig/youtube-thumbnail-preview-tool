Help options

DIY filing vs registered agent vs filing service

Use DIY when you only need the right official page. Use a paid service when you need reminders or filing help. Move to an attorney or CPA when the issue includes penalties, reinstatement, or tax questions.

Quick comparison

Option Best fit Main tradeoff When to move up a level
Do it yourself You only need the right deadline, fee, and official filing link. You are responsible for tracking every deadline and confirming every state-specific rule. Move up if you have multiple entities, multiple states, or repeated late filings.
Registered agent or compliance service You want reminders, document handling, and a compliance support layer without full legal advice. You still need to understand what is being filed and what extra fees are not included. Move up if the filing issue involves ownership changes, tax complexity, or reinstatement risk.
Business filing service You want someone to handle the mechanics of filing or entity maintenance for you. Service packages can bundle convenience items that are not required for every business. Move up if the issue affects legal structure, tax treatment, or disputes with the state.
Attorney or CPA You are dealing with legal exposure, tax planning, penalties, reinstatement, or a messy fact pattern. Higher cost and a narrower role than a routine filing service. Use this level when the downside of getting it wrong is materially larger than the cost of advice.

How to choose

  • DIY is enough when you have one entity, one state, and a clear official filing page.
  • A service makes sense when you need reminders, document handling, or multi-state tracking.
  • Move to an attorney or CPA when the issue includes reinstatement, penalties, ownership changes, or tax questions.
  • Compare the official state fee with the service fee before you pay anyone.
