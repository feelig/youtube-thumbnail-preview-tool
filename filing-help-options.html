Help options

DIY filing vs registered agent vs filing service

The right filing help depends on where the risk is coming from. Some business owners simply need the correct state page and can file on their own in minutes. Others are not confused about the fee at all; they need a reminder system, a mailing address, entity management help, or someone to keep several states straight at the same time.

This page is not a list of recommended providers. It is a framework for deciding what kind of help actually matches the problem before you spend money.

Comparison table

Option Best fit Main tradeoff When to move up a level
Do it yourself You only need the right deadline, fee, and official filing link. You are responsible for tracking every deadline and confirming every state-specific rule. Move up if you have multiple entities, multiple states, or repeated late filings.
Registered agent or compliance service You want reminders, document handling, and a compliance support layer without full legal advice. You still need to understand what is being filed and what extra fees are not included. Move up if the filing issue involves ownership changes, tax complexity, or reinstatement risk.
Business filing service You want someone to handle the mechanics of filing or entity maintenance for you. Service packages can bundle convenience items that are not required for every business. Move up if the issue affects legal structure, tax treatment, or disputes with the state.
Attorney or CPA You are dealing with legal exposure, tax planning, penalties, reinstatement, or a messy fact pattern. Higher cost and a narrower role than a routine filing service. Use this level when the downside of getting it wrong is materially larger than the cost of advice.

When DIY is enough

  • You have one entity in one state.
  • The state page clearly publishes the due date and filing fee.
  • You are not already late or in forfeiture, cancellation, or reinstatement territory.
  • You are comfortable using the official state portal yourself.

When a service starts to make sense

  • You have recurring deadlines in multiple states.
  • You need a registered-agent address or document handling help.
  • You tend to miss annual filing windows or renewal deadlines.
  • You want a single place to manage routine compliance tasks.

When not to rely on a generic service page

If the issue involves reinstatement after forfeiture, unresolved penalty notices, tax classification questions, or major ownership changes, a generic filing package may not be the right answer. Those situations can cross into legal or tax advice quickly, and a state summary page should not pretend otherwise.

How this helps the rest of the site

FinLogic Hub's state pages are designed to answer the first layer of the problem: deadline, fee, label, and official source. This page handles the next layer: whether that information is enough for you to file confidently or whether you need help from a service provider or adviser.
